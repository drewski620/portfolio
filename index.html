My Life

My name is Andrew Russ Werner and I am 29 years old. I grew up in Pensacola, Florida—and played sports throughout my youth. Everything from baseball, football, soccer—but my true love has always been sailing. The combination of technical skills and adapting to sporadic changes in nature/the environment has always been a fun challenge.

After spending multiple years working as a sailing instructor for children age 5–17, I moved to Denver when the opportunity arose to move away from my hometown. Denver is where I met the love of my life and fiancée, Erin (who also happens to be a web designer). In the process, I adopted two more fur babies, in addition to my senior pug.

In Colorado, I found my way to WeatherNation, an American broadcast, digital streaming, cable, and satellite television network. There I worked for almost two years building their studio in Centennial, CO. I learned various construction-related skills such as laying tile, grout (which I had to learn via YouTube tutorials), drywalling, firewalls, and even a refurbished wooden staircase. I also learned some technical skills like building light fixtures, green screens, giant electronic decks, and installing a ventilation system for a server room. After time passed, I went back into the service industry for a couple of years. I felt a bit burnt out within that industry, and didn't like the feeling of not having a craft or skill to offer. After thinking very in-depth about it, I decided to take the full-stack web development boot camp at the University of Denver.

This specialized skill of being a web developer is a never-ending process of learning new materials and concepts. I think the idea of web development constantly evolving is what attracts me to it. I'm extremely confident with the front end side of development, including HTML, CSS, React.js, and Javascript.

Besides the projects highlighted here, I have a ton of ideas and personal passion projects that I plan on working on in my spare time and am excited to showcase them once I get them to a shareable state. A few of these ideas include fantasy football apps, 25 and older competitive video gamer websites, and rebuilding my family's custom framing business's website.

I am currently seeking work, so please don't hesitate to get in contact if you'd like to collaborate or know of any great companies that are hiring!
